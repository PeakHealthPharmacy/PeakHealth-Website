Confidential Men's Health Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my appointment completely confidential?

Yes. All consultations take place in a private room and everything discussed is treated with full confidentiality in line with our professional obligations. Nothing is shared without your permission, and our team is trained to handle men's health enquiries with discretion and respect.

Do I need a GP referral?

No. You can book directly with us without seeing your GP first. No referral letter, no prior diagnosis and no awkward conversations at a GP reception desk. Simply book online or call us on 0141 773 2003.

What happens during the consultation?

Your pharmacist will discuss your symptoms, general health and relevant medical history. They'll also review your current medications to check for any interactions and assess whether your preferred treatment is safe and suitable for you. The appointment typically takes around 15 minutes and takes place in a private room.

What treatments are available?

For erectile dysfunction, treatment options may include sildenafil (such as Viagra) or tadalafil (such as Cialis), discussed and supplied following your consultation. For hair loss, both minoxidil (topical) and finasteride are available where clinically appropriate following pharmacist assessment. Treatment for premature ejaculation is also available following assessment. Prostate health and male fertility screening kits are available with included follow-up consultations. Your pharmacist will always recommend the most suitable option for your individual circumstances.

How long does the appointment take?

Most appointments take approximately 15 minutes. Follow-up appointments — for example to discuss your PSA or fertility screening results — are also around 15 minutes and are included in the price of your screening.

Is there a separate consultation fee?

No. The prices listed on this page include your pharmacist consultation, assessment and treatment or test kit where applicable. There are no hidden fees and no additional charge for our time and expertise.

What is the PSA test and who should consider it?

The PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) test is a simple finger-prick blood test that measures a protein produced by the prostate gland. Elevated levels can sometimes indicate a prostate condition and may prompt further investigation. It is particularly worth considering for men over 50, or earlier if you have a family history of prostate conditions. Our £14.99 service includes the test and a follow-up consultation to discuss your results.

I feel embarrassed — will it be awkward?

This is probably the most common thing men worry about before they walk through our door — and it's one of the reasons PeakHealth was built the way it was. Kez and Harvie created this service specifically so people could have the conversations they've been putting off. Your pharmacist won't make you feel awkward. They've had these conversations many times before, and their only focus is making you feel comfortable and getting you the right support. The hardest part is booking the appointment. Everything after that is easier than you expect.
